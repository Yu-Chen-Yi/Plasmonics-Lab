什麼是超穎元件 ?

What Are Metadevices ?

超穎元件（metadevice）是一種新興的人造光學元件，通常由數百萬個單元結構（或稱奈米天線）組成。這些單元結構的尺寸通常處於次波長等級，其中「次波長」一詞並非用來形容某種電磁波的波長，而是用於形容極微小的材料結構，其尺寸小於操作光波長的半個波長。在奈米光學中，當設計的結構尺寸小於作用於該材料的光波長時，這種結構表現出的特性無法單純以古典電磁理論解釋。在自然界中，也存在許多相對於可見光波長的次波長結構，例如烏鴉的羽毛在特定角度的光線照射下會展現出彩虹般的色彩，或者部分金龜子的甲殼只會反射特定偏振方向的光線，使得甲殼的色澤隨著入射光的狀態而改變。通過精密排列這些單元結構，可以製造出具有應用價值的超穎元件，如超穎透鏡、天線系統、感測器、光纖內視鏡等。

Metadevices are an emerging type of artificial optical device, typically composed of millions of unit structures (also known as nanoantennas). The size of these unit structures is generally at the subwavelength scale. The term "subwavelength" does not describe a specific electromagnetic wavelength but instead refers to extremely small material structures whose dimensions are smaller than half the wavelength of the operating light. In nanophotonics, when the designed structure size is smaller than the wavelength of light interacting with the material, the behavior exhibited by this structure cannot be fully explained by classical electromagnetic theory. In nature, many subwavelength structures exist relative to the wavelength of visible light. For instance, the feathers of a crow exhibit rainbow-like colors when illuminated at specific angles, or the exoskeletons of some beetles reflect only light with certain polarization directions, causing the shell's color to change based on the state of the incident light. By precisely arranging these unit structures, it is possible to fabricate metadevices with practical applications, such as metalenses, antenna systems, sensors, and fiber optic endoscopes.

超穎透鏡V.S.傳統透鏡

Metalens V.S. Traditional Lens

超穎透鏡的單元結構數量眾多且尺寸小，在製作上無法仰賴傳統透鏡的研磨與塑膠射出的製程方法，取而代之的是使用半導體製程的黃光微影及乾式蝕刻製程，使用半導體製程的最大好處是能大量且快速的生產超穎透鏡，且半導體製程的精準度都在奈米等級，因此能製作出高精度的超穎透鏡。超穎透鏡可以具有單片透鏡消除球差色差的能力，而傳統透鏡則需要多片透鏡才可以消除球差及色差，因此利用超穎透鏡所設計出的鏡頭模組更能維持輕薄的特徵。

Metalenses consist of a large number of unit structures with small dimensions, making traditional lens manufacturing methods such as grinding and plastic injection unsuitable. Instead, they rely on semiconductor processes like photolithography and dry etching. The biggest advantage of using semiconductor processes is the ability to mass-produce metalenses rapidly and with high precision at the nanoscale. Metalenses can eliminate spherical and chromatic aberrations with a single lens, whereas traditional lenses require multiple elements to achieve the same effect. As a result, camera modules designed with metalenses can better maintain a lightweight and slim profile.

輕薄

Lightweight and Thin

傳統凸透鏡在光學聚焦過程中主要依賴於透鏡的曲率。這類透鏡的中心部分較為厚實，而邊緣部分相對較薄，光線進入透鏡後被折射，形成一個聚焦點。因此，傳統凸透鏡的設計通常顯得笨重，擁有相對較大的厚度。為了改善這種厚重的問題，菲涅爾透鏡應運而生。菲涅爾透鏡通過將透鏡劃分為一系列理論上無窮多的同心圓紋路，即菲涅爾帶，以實現相同的光學效果。這種設計節省了材料的使用，因為透鏡的總體厚度因此減小，厚度可達毫米等級。相較之下，超穎透鏡則由眾多微小結構組成，並以繞射的方式實現透鏡的聚焦效果。這種結構使得超穎透鏡的厚度能夠達到微米等級。由於這種繞射的設計，超穎透鏡的厚度更為細薄，不僅減輕了重量，也減少了對材料的需求。

Traditional convex lenses primarily rely on curvature for optical focusing. These lenses tend to have a thicker center and thinner edges, refracting light to form a focal point. Consequently, traditional convex lenses are often bulky with relatively large thicknesses. To address this issue, Fresnel lenses were developed. Fresnel lenses divide the lens into an infinite series of concentric circular grooves, known as Fresnel zones, achieving the same optical effect. This design reduces material usage, significantly decreasing the overall thickness to the millimeter scale. In contrast, metalenses consist of numerous microscopic structures and achieve focusing through diffraction. This structure allows metalenses to reach thicknesses at the micrometer scale. Due to this diffractive design, metalenses are not only thinner but also lighter, reducing both weight and material requirements.

色散

Dispersion

超穎透鏡是透過單一片透鏡結構，並由眾多共振單元組成，以巧妙的折射率調節來實現色散的有效消除。這種透鏡利用特殊的材料或結構，使得不同波長的光在透鏡中獲得相似的折射率，進而極大地降低色散效應。此外，超穎透鏡的非球面形狀採用了非傳統的設計，有助於實現各個波長的光在透鏡中的均勻焦距，進一步減小了色散的影響。光學異向性也被精心應用，利用材料在不同方向上的折射率變化，以實現在不同波長下更均勻的焦點。

Metalenses achieve effective dispersion elimination through a single lens structure composed of numerous resonant units. By skillfully adjusting the refractive index, these lenses leverage special materials or structures to ensure that different wavelengths of light experience similar refractive indices within the lens, thereby significantly reducing dispersion effects. Moreover, the aspherical shape of the metalens employs a nontraditional design, which helps maintain uniform focal lengths for various wavelengths, further minimizing the impact of dispersion. Optical anisotropy is also meticulously applied, utilizing the variation in refractive indices in different directions to achieve more uniform focal points across different wavelengths.

結構由眾多共振單元精心組成，這些共振單元在設計階段就被優化以實現超穎透鏡的消球差與像差效果，下圖是我們開發能消除像差的超穎透鏡之聚焦點大小，從量測的結果來看，有消除像差能力的超穎透鏡其聚焦點接近理論上的繞射極限，提供更佳的成像品質。

The structure is meticulously composed of numerous resonant units, which are optimized during the design phase to achieve spherical and aberration correction in metalenses. D/MOE (Face ID)

D/MOE (Face ID)

近年智慧型手機和各種穿戴式裝置的興起，生物識別技術便是其中熱門的話題之一，透過對裝置的使用者進行面部辨識(Face ID)，比起傳統的輸入密碼和指紋解鎖更可以快速識別且更高程度的保障使用者個人資料。而Face ID 的運作方式是使用點陣投影器和 3D 深度感測器來識別裝置使用者的面部資訊，其中的點陣投影器(Dot projector)是透過手機內部的紅外線經過繞射元件(DOE)後產生的繞射點投影在人臉上。

In recent years, the rise of smartphones and various wearable devices has brought biometric technologies to the forefront as a popular topic. Face recognition (Face ID) enables faster identification and offers higher levels of personal data protection compared to traditional password input and fingerprint unlocking. Face ID operates by using a dot projector and a 3D depth sensor to recognize the facial features of the device's user. The dot projector generates a diffraction dot pattern on the face through the infrared light within the device passing through a diffraction optical element (DOE).

DOE利用微細結構改變光的相位或幾何形狀，以實現各種光學功能，包括光束整形、波前調制、輕量化光學系統和多波長操作，廣泛應用於雷射技術、干涉儀器、通信等領域。

DOEs use microstructures to alter the phase or geometry of light, enabling various optical functions, including beam shaping, wavefront modulation, lightweight optical systems, and multi-wavelength operation. These elements are widely applied in laser technologies, interferometric instruments, communication systems, and more.

傳統的點陣投影器主要是由垂直腔表面發射雷射陣列(VCSEL)、準直透鏡和DOE所組成，需要透過中間的準直透鏡將光變成平行光後再入射DOE，而中間的準直透鏡通常為塑膠材料，容易因為時間黃化而使整體的繞射效率降低。相比之下MOE(Meta-Optic Element)便可以解決此問題。

Traditional dot projectors are mainly composed of a vertical-cavity surface-emitting laser array (VCSEL), a collimating lens, and a DOE. The collimating lens in the middle is used to convert light into parallel beams before entering the DOE. However, the collimating lens is typically made of plastic, which tends to yellow over time, reducing the overall diffraction efficiency. In contrast, MOEs (Meta-Optic Elements) can address this issue effectively.
